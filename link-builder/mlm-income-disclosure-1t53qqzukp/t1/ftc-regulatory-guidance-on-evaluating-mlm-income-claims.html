FTC Regulatory Guidance On Evaluating MLM Income Claims

The Federal Trade Commission treats the income representations made by multi-level marketing companies as advertising claims, which means they fall under the general prohibition on unfair or deceptive practices in the FTC Act. The practical consequence is that any earnings or lifestyle claim a company or its distributors make must be truthful, not misleading, and backed by evidence before it is made. FTC guidance directed at the MLM industry has emphasized that if a company presents potential income figures, those figures should reflect what participants can generally expect to earn rather than the atypical results of a small number of top performers, and that misleading impressions can be created not only by explicit numbers but also by images of wealth, testimonials, and other cues..

The guidance also frames how such claims should be evaluated in context. A disclaimer noting that "results are not typical," for example, does not cure an otherwise deceptive impression if the overall message still suggests that meaningful income is likely. Regulators look at the net impression an ordinary consumer would take away, so the omission of relevant information — such as the share of participants who earn little or nothing, or costs that offset gross earnings — can itself be deceptive even when no individual statement is literally false. The FTC has also signaled that it can seek penalties against companies that continue to make earnings claims it has previously identified as deceptive..
